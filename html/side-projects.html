The Forgotten Island

Synopsis?

This is the tricky part... The original idea (had back in 2017 at my sister's wedding) was this: a family was caught and cast out of society into the Pariah Zone. Before the family was caught, the mother gave her son secretly to a friend to take care of and raise. Unbeknownst to him, the boy has unparalleled magical powers. Join him as he discovers his powers, his past, and the plot to overthrow an empire eons old.

I don't know if the book will follow that exact premise, but that's the gist. A corrupt government who creates outcast scapegoats will be overthrown by a seeming nobody with immense power & potential. Oh, did I mention the government ruler has this same power & potential and has been secretly committing genocide to prevent its widespread use that could threaten his throne?? Yeah, it's going to be wild.

ETA?

With school and work and family taking up a lot of my time, I don't know when I will actually get around to writing the book. The time I spend now consists of creating the world map, outlining the general plot, and creating characters (scripting them, naming them, visualizing them). I'm hoping to have a rough draft done by 2027, and this might end up as a 3-4 book series if I can have my way with it.

What's been done so far?

As I mentioned, not a lot of time is able to be devoted to one of my lifetime goals of writing a novel/series yet, so these are the minor things that have been completed so far.

  • Map Creation - General lands & terrains
  • Outlining the Plot
  • Creating a world - outlining societal roles & functions, government structure,
  • Magic system(s) (there is likely going to be more than 1 at play) - functions, uses, and how to obtain & increase skills

Pi-Hole

What is Pi-Hole?

This is too much for me to explain. Go to the Wiki page for PiHole for more detailed info.

TLDR - PiHole is an ad-blocker and allows for superior control over internet traffic and use in the home. It is open sourced and cool if you can figure it out.

How to do it?

Here are some YouTube videos and links explaining how to do it! You need a raspberry or some other computer that runs Linux. This computer will be running constantly so choose something that uses minimal power (i.e. raspberry pi).

  • Here is the Pi-Hole Website.

Once you've fully set up the raspberry pi, Wi-Fi configuration settings, and pi-hole, you can start blocking what you want! Here are some links to blacklists for what I block and why; the rest you can google around for! These aren't all navigable links, so I'll put the in as text, and you can either add them to your ad-list (of things to blacklist) or go to them by copying and pasting.

  • General - https://raw.githubusercontent.com/StevenBlack/hosts/master/hosts
  • Tracking - https://s3.amazonaws.com/lists.disconnect.me/simple_tracking.txt
  • Ads - https://s3.amazonaws.com/lists.disconnect.me/simple_ad.txt
  • Adult Content - https://raw.githubusercontent.com/chadmayfield/my-pihole-blocklists/master/lists/pi_blocklist_porn_all.list
  • General - http://sysctl.org/cameleon/hosts
  • SmartTV - https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Perflyst/PiHoleBlocklist/master/SmartTV.txt
  • LG Tracking - https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Perflyst/PiHoleBlocklist/master/regex.list
  • Safe Adlist - https://dbl.oisd.nl/

Home Server

What is Home Serving/Self Hosting?

The concept in which you host your own applications, data, and more. Taking away the "unknown" factor in how your data is managed and stored, this provides those with the willingness to learn and the mind to do so to take control of their data without losing the functionality of services they otherwise use frequently.
Some Examples: For instance, if you use dropbox, but are not fond of having your most sensitive data stored in a data-storage container that you do not have direct control over, you may consider NextCloud Of let's say you're used to hosting a blog out of a Blogger platform, but would rather have your own customization and flexibility of controlling your updates? Why not give WordPress a go. The possibilities are endless and it all starts here with a server.

How to do it?

I'm not entirely sure on the whole process. The Wiki Page for self-hosting has good information. Also, the subreddits r/selfhosted and r/HomeServer have lots of information and places to interact w/ community members who've paved the way and done this before.
